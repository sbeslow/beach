Ecoli

E-coli is a bacteria found in the intestines of animals. The city monitors the amount of E-coli in the water as an indicator for the amount of fecal contamination. The primary source of E-coli at Chicago beaches are animals, sewers, and garbage.
Fecal contamination can cause diarrhea, abdominal cramping, pain or tenderness, nausea, and vomiting


Where Does Drek Beach Data Come From?


The Chicago Park District beach website currently lists today's swim advisories, today's predicted e-coli levels, and yesterdays e-coli measurements.

Drek Beach has been monitoring this website since the beaches opened on May 22, 2015.

As a civic hacker, open government advocate, and beach lover, I wanted to create a website dedicated to exploring the resulting data.

Check out the Drek Beach GitHub repo to open issues, see the code, or access data.
